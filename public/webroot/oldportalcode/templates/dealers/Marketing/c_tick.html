Marketing Tip

Subject - `C' Tick

  There have been an increasing number of enquiries from our dealers concerning the impact of the EMC compatibility framework and `C' tick. It would seem that many dealers have little or no knowledge of this subject and are rightly concerned about the affect of the regulations on our business. To assist in the understanding of our responsibilities I outline below some of the relevant matters. I would stress that each of our dealers should take steps to arm themselves with the various official publications and that they should use the services of their local offices to clarify any areas of doubt that they may have.
General The Australian EMC Framework was established to provide a basic level of protection from interference to the radio frequency spectrum in Australia. Fundamental to the framework is the principle of industry self regulation which places responsibility on the Australian supplier for the products placed on the market.
Standards The EMC Framework is based upon a set of standards, made into law by the Spectrum Management Agency (SMA), that have been adapted from international standards. The standards applying to our industry are AS/NZS 2064.1/2 ; AS/NZS 3458 & AS/NZS 4251.1
Suppliers Responsibility Under the EMC Framework it is the responsibility of suppliers - manufacturers or product importers - to ensure that products placed on the Australian market satisfy the technical requirements of the EMC Framework.

Suppliers must satisfy 4 basic requirements under the EMC Framework:

They must:

Establish sound technical grounds for product compliance;

Make and hold a Declaration of Conformity;

Prepare and keep a Compliance Folder

Label the product as directed.

Once these basic requirements have been satisfied a product can be offered for sale.
